About Me

My very first camera was given to me on my seventh birthday, where you only get one flash bulb and hope for the best. I became fasinated about photography, the experiences, and the magic it holds.

My uncle, who owned his own photo store, upgraded me from point and shoot, to his old, manual, heavy camera, with fixed lenes, and a flash, as a present. It was in junior year of high school, where I started to photograph the environment around me, which was local punk shows, my surrounding neighborhoods, and my friends.

In college, I spent my weekends in the darkroom, exploring with different chemicals, manipulations, film devlopment, and printing. It was a magical experience, to be in control, and learned to fail when film was not developed correctly, and all my memories of the present time, were earsed.

Now I have the tools to have endless frames, but I try to be present how I was taught, just to be in one.
